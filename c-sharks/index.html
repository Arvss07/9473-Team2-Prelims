Save our Seas – Protect Life Beneath the Breeze

Our ocean is the lifeblood of our planet, comprising the largest portion of the Earth's surface, accounting for 70%. Our seas regulates the climate, provide livelihood for millions of people worldwide, and are home to an incredible diversity of marine life.

The Beauty of Philippine Seas

In the Philippines, our ocean is deeply tied with our way of life as Filipinos. With over 7,600 islands and approximately 36,000 kilometers of coastline, the sea has shaped our Filipino culture, including but not limited to our cuisines, and it also affects our economy for generations.

The Rich Marine Biodiversity

Additionally, The Philippine geography sits on the Coral Triangle, which is an area that has the highest marine biodiversity on earth, with 75 percent of the world's coral species are found here—nearly 600 different species and 37 percent reef fish species [1]

The Oceanic Lungs

Scientists estimate that between 70 and 80 percent of the oxygen we breathe is produced by marine plants found in our ocean and nearly all of which are marine algae. These tiny ocean dwellers are working overtime to keep our atmosphere breathable, highlighting the critical role our oceans play in sustaining life on Earth.

Oceans as Resources

Oceans are our planet’s life support as they provide water, food and help regulate the weather [2]. The ocean provides natural resources including materials, substances, energy and they also contribute to poverty reduction.

Rising Tides, Rising Concerns

However, our oceans are under threat. Marine ecosystems are suffering due to overfishing, pollution, habitat degradation, and climate change, both in the Philippines and globally. To save the oceans for present and future generations, we need to take action right away.
